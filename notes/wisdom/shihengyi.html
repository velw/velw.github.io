Shi Heng Yi

Shi Heng Yi (释恒義) is a Shaolin Master and the headmaster of Shaolin Temple Europe near the city of Kaiserslautern in Germany.

Lineage

Shi Heng Yi began studying the Shaolin arts at the age of four. He passed the master exam at the age of 18 and belongs to the 35th generation of Shaolin masters.[1][2]

Shi Heng Yi received his religious name in 2004 from Shi Yan Hai of the 34th generation of Shaolin masters.[3] Shi Yan Hai studied at the Shaolin Monastery in Henan Province, China, under Shi Yong Xin, the monastery’s current abbot.[4]

The Shaolin arts of Kung Fu, Qi Gong and Tai Ji were first taught in Kaiserslautern in 1983. The Shaolin Temple was officially established there in 1997 under the supervision of later abbot Shi Heng Zong and in coordination with the Shaolin Monastery.[3] The Shaolin Temple in Kaiserslautern split from the Shaolin Monastery in 2008.[5]

About Shaolin

The Shaolin Monastery is the centre of Chan Buddhism, a Chinese school of Mahayana Buddhism known in Japan and elsewhere as Zen.

The first abbot of the Shaolin Monastery was dhyana master Buddhabhadra, known in China as Batuo, who is said to have travelled from Southern India to China in 464AD and preached Nikaya Buddhism there for 30 years before the Shaolin Monastery was established for that purpose.

Bodhidharma, a legendary Buddhist monk credited with transmitting Buddhism to China, is said to have begun the physical training of the monks at the Shaolin Monastery that led to the creation of Shaolin Kung Fu. Bodhidharma is said to be the 28th Patriarch of Buddhism in an uninterrupted line that extends back to Gautama Buddha.
